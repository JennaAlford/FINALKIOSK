ABOUT

The digital afterlife shapes how we remember, honor, and interact with those who have passed. As our lives become increasingly connected to digital platforms, the traces we leave behind continue to influence family, friends, communities, and even cultural memory. This project explores how memorial practices have evolved in the digital age and how they intersect with religious understandings of death, mourning, and the afterlife. Across this interactive experience, you will see highlights of digital memorial projects used within Christianity, Judaism, and Islam, each offering a unique perspective on how tradition adapts to new technological landscapes.
